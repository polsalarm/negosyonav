The problem
Registration is a one-time event. The app can't be.
Once a user completes their Barangay Clearance, DTI, Mayor's Permit, and BIR registration, there's no obvious reason to open the app again. This is the single biggest product risk for NegosyoNav. Here's how to solve it at each time horizon.
Short term · During registration (weeks 1–8)
Registration takes weeks, not a day — the roadmap is already a multi-session feature.
The average MSME registration process across all four steps takes 2–6 weeks of back-and-forth visits. The Lakad Roadmap is inherently a multi-session experience. Progress is saved to Firestore, so every incomplete step is a reason to return.
Trigger
Saved progress
Firestore saves completed steps. Next session opens exactly where they left off — like an order tracker.
Trigger
PDF re-download
User loses or crumples their printout. They return to NegosyoNav to re-download the pre-filled form — not start over on a blank sheet.
Trigger
Step-failed notification
"Naalala mo ba? 3 araw ka nang nakatigil sa DTI step." Gentle push nudge after inactivity.
Medium term · Post-registration (months 1–6)
"Registered ka na — ano ang susunod?"
Registration is just the beginning of running a business. There are 5–7 immediate follow-up steps that new registrants always need. NegosyoNav becomes the "what's next" guide for each of these — every one is a new Lakad Roadmap.
Open a business bank account
New roadmap: requirements per bank (BDO, UnionBank, Landbank for gov't vendors). Very high demand — vendors always ask this right after BIR registration.
SSS / PhilHealth / Pag-IBIG employer registration
If they plan to hire kahit isang helper. Triggers a new roadmap with pre-filled employer forms.
BIR quarterly filing guide
First quarterly filing is 90 days after BIR registration. Most new registrants have no idea how to do this — huge pain point and high search volume.
Official Receipt (OR) printing
BIR-accredited printers, ATP application — every new registrant needs this and doesn't know the process.
DTI trademark registration
Protecting the business name. Small step, big peace of mind. New roadmap.
Long term · Ongoing (year 1+)
Annual renewals + the Hub turn NegosyoNav into a permanent companion.
After the first year, the app becomes a compliance calendar. Every registered business in the Philippines has recurring obligations — and most small vendors miss deadlines because nobody reminds them.
January every year
Mayor's Permit renewal
Push notification 2 weeks before. Pre-fills the renewal form with their saved data. They just re-download and go.
Quarterly
BIR filing reminders
April, July, October, January. Most micro-entrepreneurs miss these. NegosyoNav becomes the only reminder they have.
Ongoing
New grant cycles
LGU and DOLE programs open at different times of year. Push alert when a new program matching their profile opens. Brings them back without them searching.
Ongoing
Negosyante Hub
If someone replies to their post or upvotes their tip, they return. Community reciprocity is the strongest organic retention mechanic available.
The honest answer for judges
The PDF download is actually the first retention hook.
A user who downloads a pre-filled DTI form has now stored value in the app — their data is there. When they need to re-download because they lost the printout, when the Mayor's Permit renewal comes around and their info is already pre-filled, when they need the BIR form for quarterly filing — the saved profile is the reason to return. The form auto-fill feature isn't just a convenience: it's the data anchor that makes NegosyoNav worth keeping installed.
