NegosyoNav pitch brief — problem, solution, and features overview

NegosyoNav

Pitch Brief

Lowering the cost of dreaming for Filipino micro-entrepreneurs — one step at a time.

The Problem

Formalization is a maze — and MSMEs pay for it with their time, money, and future.

⏳

Time lost closing the shop

A carinderia owner in Sampaloc has to fall in line at the barangay, DTI, City Hall, and BIR — often on separate days, with no clear order of steps.

💸

Money wasted on fixers

Confusing jargon and inconsistent requirements push small vendors to pay fixers just to understand a process they could navigate themselves.

📍

The process is NOT the same everywhere

A business in Taguig has different LGU requirements, offices, and forms than one in Cavite or Sampaloc. Generic guides fail them.

📉

MSMEs fail before they even start

Without knowing they qualify for LGU micro-financing, DOLE livelihood programs, or Negosyo Center grants, many just give up — or operate informally with legal risk.

The Core System Problem

The system wasn't designed for the people who need it most.

Philippine MSME formalization processes were built for businesses with lawyers, accountants, and administrative staff. The system assumes you have time to research, money to spare, and fluency in legal English. Small vendors — sari-sari store owners, street food entrepreneurs, home-based crafters — have none of these. The result: high informal sector rates, missed government support, and a perpetuating "cost of dreaming" where ambition is punished by bureaucracy.

"Gusto ko sana mag-rehistro ng carinderia sa Sampaloc pero hindi ko alam kung saan magsisimula."
Specific beneficiary: micro-entrepreneurs Systemic barrier: bureaucratic complexity Cost of dreaming: time + money + opportunity

The Solution

NegosyoNav — a personalized, location-aware guide that turns bureaucracy into a manageable checklist.

Instead of a static government page, NegosyoNav is a conversational, AI-powered workflow that meets the user where they are — in their language, their barangay, and their schedule.

User chats in Taglish → AI builds Lakad Roadmap → Forms auto-filled → Grants matched → Community validates

Core Features

Eight pillars, built for real Philippine conditions.

01 · AI Intake

Taglish Conversational Chat

User describes their business in natural language — Filipino, Taglish, or English. No forms, no jargon upfront.

02 · Roadmap

Location-Aware Lakad Roadmap

Personalized step-by-step guide based on LGU — Taguig, Cavite, Sampaloc, etc. — with exact offices, windows, and requirements.

03 · Automation

Form Auto-Fill

Pulls user input and pre-populates DTI, Barangay Clearance, and BIR forms — print-ready. No need to re-enter data.

04 · Matching

Grant & Livelihood Matching

Checks profile against LGU grants, DOLE livelihood programs, and Negosyo Center funds. Alerts user if they qualify.

05 · Community

Negosyante Hub

Reddit-style community board. Real vendors share tips, warn about fixers, post actual experiences at their LGU — peer-to-peer truth.

06 · Scheduling

Time-Based Task Planner

"Mayroon ka bang 2 oras ngayon?" AI suggests which steps you can realistically complete today based on your available time.

07 · Navigation

Smart Place Finder

Google Maps integration shows the nearest DTI Negosyo Center, BIR RDO, or City Hall — with queue tips and best times to go.

08 · Costs

Registration Cost Estimator

Breaks down every fee — DTI registration (₱200-₱2,000), Mayor's Permit, BIR ARF — so vendors can budget before they queue.
